Section 1 — The shift

General-purpose LLMs don't speak bank.

One string. Many meanings.

SQ *COFFEE 12345 SAN FRA CA
SQUARE COFFEE 12345*
SQ Coffee #1 SF 4154...
PURCHASE SQ COFFEE BAR
resolves to →
merchant: "Square (Coffee Bar)" · category: FOOD_AND_DRINK_COFFEE
01

Bank descriptions appear in dozens of string variations — for the same merchant, the same charge.

02

The same string can mean different things depending on context and shifting institutional metadata.

03

Layering a general-purpose LLM on top doesn't fix the underlying ambiguity — it inherits it.

What's needed is a shared, scalable representation of financial activity that works across institutions, products, and use cases.
