KeyBank — Small Business Account Funding — Fund a new business checking account from any bank in seconds, post-KYB, with ownership + ACH-risk checks built in
================================================================================

**Host:** **KeyBank** — U.S. business banking (small-business deposit account funding)
**Canonical URL:** https://www.key.com
**Brand URL:** https://www.key.com

**User journey (one sentence):**
A small-business owner who has just completed KYB at KeyBank funds their new business checking account from the company's external bank by connecting it with Plaid, and KeyBank instantly confirms the account belongs to the business, pulls the ACH rails, and clears the transfer for ACH-return risk before approving the funds.

**Story arc (short prose):**
KYB is done — the business is verified and its details are on file. The friction is now *funding*: slow micro-deposits and the risk of moving money into the wrong account. KeyBank drops in Plaid: the owner links the company's external bank in one tap, and behind the scenes KeyBank confirms the linked account's name matches the business (Identity Match), pulls account + routing rails and sees it's a business account (Auth), captures the amount, and risk-scores that specific transfer (Signal). Low risk → funds approved instantly, no micro-deposits. Close on the value: small businesses funded and transacting on day one, with fewer returns and less fraud.

**Slide content (required):**
- **identity-match-slide** (JSON rail mirrors `/identity/match`):
  - Company name: onboarding/KYB form **"Pixel & Paper Co"** vs external bank **"Pixel & Paper"** -> **`legal_name.score` 95** (fuzzy match tolerates the "Co" legal suffix).
  - The same name-scoring convention works for **company names AND person names** — e.g. **"John B. Henry"** vs **"Johnathan Henry"** still scores as a strong match (middle-initial vs full-middle, abbreviation tolerance).
  - Takeaway: formatting / suffix / abbreviation differences don't sink the score; ownership is confirmed before funds move.
- **auth-slide** (JSON rail mirrors `/auth/get`): highlight the account holder/type values — the account object's **`holder_category`** can be **`business`**, `personal`, or `unrecognized` (this account is **`business`**), alongside `type` (depository) / `subtype` (checking) and the ACH `numbers`. Show prospects Plaid distinguishes a business account on the funding rail.

**Product-specific reminders:**
- Signal: low score = low ACH return risk; happy path **ACCEPT** with low scores (customer-initiated 12, bank-initiated 10). `ruleset.result` in {ACCEPT, REVIEW, REROUTE} — happy path ACCEPT.
- Identity Match: scores 0–100; pass the business legal name as `legal_name`; ~70+ is a strong match. Never label scores "Trust Index".
- Link: the pre-Link step ends on the **button** ("…taps Link your business bank account."); the Link step narrates content **inside** the modal only.
- Consumer/host KeyBank screens never show raw JSON; JSON belongs only in the global `#api-response-panel` and the technical slides' JSON rail.
