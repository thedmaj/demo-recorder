**Business objectives (derived from Salesforce context):**
- Increase successful SMB account opening starts that reach funded status.
- Reduce drop-off caused by manual verification friction during onboarding.
- Improve confidence in identity checks by separating business-entity name matching from owner-individual name matching.
- Keep funding initiation fast while preserving risk controls.

**User journey (one sentence):**
A small business applicant opens a new U.S. Bank account, links an external funding account through Plaid Link, and is approved with clear identity-match signals for both business and owner names.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Start in U.S. Bank's SMB onboarding flow where applicants often stall at verification and initial funding. Introduce Plaid as the fast verification and funding rail: the owner consents in Link, U.S. Bank verifies account/auth data, evaluates identity match quality, and moves the account to funded with clear, explainable checks.

**Identity Match slide requirement (mandatory):**
- Include two explicit name-match dimensions in the narrative and JSON:
  - `business_name_match_score`: compares applicant-entered legal business name vs institution-derived account holder business/legal name.
  - `owner_name_match_score`: compares owner personal name vs institution-derived personal owner name.
- If business score is strong but owner score is weak (or vice versa), show policy as `REVIEW` instead of blind `PASS`.
- Do not collapse these into one generic "name score"; demonstrate why entity vs person matching matters for SMB onboarding.
