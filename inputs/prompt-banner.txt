Banner Health Post-Procedure Payment Follow-Up - Lower Card Costs with Verified ACH
================================================================================

**Host:** **Banner Health** - healthcare provider / payment operations
**Canonical URL:** https://www.bannerhealth.com/
**Brand URL:** https://www.bannerhealth.com/

**Opportunity context (Gong discovery notes):**
- Banner Health is rolling out cashless payments across 700+ locations and still relies primarily on cards and checks.
- Current payment mix is approximately 85% credit / 15% debit, with ACH not yet broadly enabled.
- Banner runs high-volume payment plans: ~182,000 plans created in 12 months, ~56,391 currently active, average plan length ~10.7 months.
- Installment collections on cards compound processing cost because each monthly installment incurs card percentage fees.
- Banner requires consistency across phone, in-person, and post-service channels, with next-day funding as a hard requirement.
- Revenue Cycle is the economic decision-maker; Treasury and Payment Operations are assembling ROI to support approval.

**Business objectives (derived from call transcript):**
- Add a trusted post-procedure ACH payment path to reduce card fee burden on installment collections.
- Preserve patient conversion with familiar, low-friction account linking and payment setup.
- Reduce ACH return risk using account verification + identity matching + risk decisioning before payment initiation.
- Produce concrete ROI talking points that support Revenue Cycle approval in Q2 planning.

**User journey (one sentence):**
After receiving a post-procedure bill, a Banner patient opens the follow-up portal, links a bank account with Plaid, and completes an ACH payment-plan enrollment backed by identity, account, and risk checks.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Begin in Banner's post-procedure payment follow-up flow where card-only plans create recurring fee drag. Banner introduces Plaid to securely connect bank accounts, verify account ownership, validate account details, and evaluate ACH return risk before enrollment. The result is a confident ACH setup that protects conversion and lowers total processing cost across long-tail installment plans.
