Citi Account Opening + Instant Funding — Inbound Bank Payments
================================================================================

Brand URL: https://www.citibank.com

**Host:** **Citi** — consumer banking / digital account opening
**Canonical URL:** https://www.citibank.com
**Brand URL** (optional): https://www.citibank.com

**User journey (one sentence):**
A new Citi customer opens a checking account online and funds it instantly by linking
an external bank account through Plaid, with risk-aware decisioning before the inbound
transfer is finalized.

**Story arc (short prose):**
A new customer starts in Citi's account-opening funnel and reaches the funding step,
where multi-day micro-deposits would normally stall activation. Citi offers external
bank funding as the fastest path to activate the account. The customer launches real
Plaid Link once, connects their existing bank, and behind the scenes Citi pulls verified
ACH rails (Auth), confirms account ownership (Identity Match), and scores ACH return
risk (Signal) — supporting a confident auto-approve of the inbound transfer. The account
is activated in seconds instead of days.

**Product-specific reminders** (delete if N/A):
- Link: exactly one pre-Link host screen and exactly one `plaidPhase: "launch"` step.
- Link: pre-step narration ends on the **button** (`Link your bank`); the Link step narrates
  content **inside** the modal only — never the act of opening it.
- Identity Match: per-attribute scores are 0–100 (70+ = Pass; 100 = exact). Surface a realistic
  mixed result (most attributes pass, one weaker) so the approval decision reads as a real judgment.
- Signal: low score = low ACH return risk. Use a low-risk **ACCEPT** path with score in the **5–20**
  range; never 82–97. `ruleset.result` ∈ {ACCEPT, REVIEW, REROUTE} — never `REJECT`.

**Compliance / user data (one line):**
Treat Identity Match and Signal outputs as policy inputs to funding risk controls (not sole
decision makers); present ACH funding as a customer-authorized inbound transfer.
