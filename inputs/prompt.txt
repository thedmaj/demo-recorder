**Host:** **Chase** — consumer credit cards / premium rewards  
**Canonical URL:** https://www.chase.com/

**Glean / internal research notes (CRA second-look):**
- Base Report = foundational CRA: up to 24 months consumer-permissioned bank data, identity, balances, categorized cash-flow, account stability signals.
- Income Insights = income add-on: historical gross/net income, forecasted income, employer, frequency, predicted next pay date — retrieved after Base Report path.
- Link: `products: ["cra_base_report","cra_income_insights"]`; user must exist via `/user/create` with identity (name, DOB, emails, phones, addresses; partial SSN recommended). Token: `consumer_report_permissible_purpose` (e.g. credit review / account review credit use case), `cra_options`, webhook; report is async (`USER_CHECK_REPORT_READY` / check report ready lifecycle) before GETs.
- Second-look narrative: applicant pended or not fully approved on bureau-only path; bank offers permissioned cash-flow + income verification to expand the credit box responsibly.

**User journey (one sentence):**  
A Chase Sapphire applicant receives a not-instant approval after the initial bureau review and is invited to link accounts through Plaid Check Link (CRA) so underwriters can complete a second look using Base Report and Income Insights before a final decision.

**Story arc (short prose):**  
Bureau-first decisioning leaves good borrowers invisible. Chase offers a clear second look: the applicant consents to Plaid Check Link, links a non-OAuth sandbox bank with a CRA test persona, and Plaid generates a consumer report. After a report-ready beat, underwriters review Base Report cash-flow context and Income Insights for ability-to-pay — ending in a confident approved outcome for the demo.
