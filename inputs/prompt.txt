**User journey (one sentence):**
A Rain member asks to split this month's rent into two payments; she connects her bank once through Plaid Link (multi-item CRA flow), Rain underwrites ability-to-pay from her CRA Base Report (cash flow) and Income Insights (verified income), approves the split aligned to her payday, screens real-time Balance before each repayment, and — months later — repairs a lapsed connection in seconds via Plaid Link update mode.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Jordan Rivera wants to split her $1,800 rent into $900 now and $900 on payday. Rain can't underwrite that from a thin credit file, so it asks her to connect her bank. In one Plaid Link session (CRA + Auth + Transactions + Balance, multi-item enabled) Rain collects FCRA-permissible consent and the bank connection. Plaid generates the Consumer Report asynchronously; when it's ready, Rain reads the CRA Base Report (365-day cash flow, zero NSFs, healthy balances) and CRA Income Insights (verified biweekly payroll, monthly net income, predicted next payday). Rain approves the rent split on a schedule aligned to her income, and before each debit it pulls a real-time Balance to avoid an insufficient-funds return. Close on the operational reality of lending: months later Jordan's bank connection lapses (item_login_required) — Rain surfaces a "reconnect" prompt and Plaid Link update mode repairs it in seconds with no re-exchange and no lost history. One connection powers underwriting, PFM, repayment, and self-healing. (Compliance-safe: "can help," "typically," "up to"; no guarantees.)
