================================================================================
Spring EQ — One-tap HELOC onboarding to cash-flow-backed approval (Plaid Layer + Plaid Consumer Report: CRA Base Report + CRA LendScore)
================================================================================

**Host:** **Spring EQ** — home equity lender (HELOCs and home equity loans)
**Brand URL:** https://www.springeq.com

**User journey (one sentence):**
A homeowner starts a HELOC application and taps once to onboard with Plaid Layer — a single session that shares their prefilled identity and permissions their bank — and Spring EQ then generates a Plaid Consumer Report (CRA) server-side to underwrite on 365 days of verified cash flow plus a CRA LendScore, pre-qualifying the line in minutes.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Gregory Hudson wants a $75,000 HELOC against his home. Spring EQ's digital funnel loses applicants to long forms, so it leads with Plaid Layer: in one tap Gregory reviews his prefilled name, email, phone, and property address AND permissions his bank — a single Layer session, no second connection step. Spring EQ writes that identity to the Plaid user record and generates his Consumer Report server-side. It then reads the **CRA Base Report** (365-day cash flow — healthy inflows, zero NSFs, stable balances) and a **CRA LendScore** (1–99, higher = lower default risk) as a complementary cash-flow risk signal, and pre-qualifies the line. Compliance-safe language only ("can help," "typically," "up to").

**Product-specific reminders:**
- ONE launch only (Layer). The report-generation beat (step 3) is a server-side async state, NOT a `plaidPhase` launch.
- LendScore: 1–99, **higher = lower default risk = safer** (opposite of Plaid Signal). Predicts 12-month non-mortgage default risk; here a complementary cash-flow signal, never the sole basis. Retrieved via `/cra/check_report/lend_score/get` after report-ready; not a Link product string.
- Layer identity is user-permissioned; written to the Plaid user record via `/user/update` before report generation. Frame as "shared / permissioned," not document-verified KYC.
- CRA reports retrieved by `user_id`.
- Compliance-safe claims only: "can help," "typically," "up to"; never "guarantee" / "eliminate." Avoid GTM percentage stats in narration.
- Happy path only — no declines/errors.

**Compliance / user data:** FCRA consumer-report consent + permissible purpose (`EXTENSION_OF_CREDIT`) collected in the Layer/CRA session; representative sandbox data.
