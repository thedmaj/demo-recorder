**Business objectives (Gong + marketplace context):**
- Credit Karma surfaces financial product offers to millions of members; when members fund accounts or move money via ACH, return risk erodes conversion and partner economics.
- Upsell evaluates **Plaid Signal** to score ACH debit risk at transfer time (0–99, higher = higher return risk) and route low-risk users to faster funding.
- Pair with existing Plaid Link + Auth footprint; happy path shows **ACCEPT** with a low Signal score (5–20 range per pipeline rules).

**User journey (one sentence):**
A Credit Karma member links her checking account to fund a new savings goal, Plaid Signal scores the ACH transfer as low risk, and she sees funds available in seconds instead of waiting for settlement uncertainty.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Jordan checks her Credit Karma dashboard and starts a transfer to fund a high-yield savings bucket. She taps Link Your Bank on the pre-Link screen. Inside Plaid Link she confirms with OTP, selects First Platypus Bank checking, and connects. On the transfer review screen, Credit Karma calls Signal: score **14 — ACCEPT**, bank balance sufficient, rules engine green-lights instant availability. She sees "Available now" with a collapsed API panel showing `signal/evaluate` attributes. A closing beat quantifies fewer ACH returns and higher funded conversion for CK's money-movement flows.
