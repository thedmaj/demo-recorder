================================================================================
Ascend — Approve the thin file on cash flow (CRA Base Report + Income Insights + Network Insights + LendScore)
================================================================================

**Host:** **Ascend** — short-term, small-dollar consumer lender (fictional)

**User journey (one sentence):**
A thin-file applicant applies for a small short-term loan and connects their bank once through Plaid Link with FCRA-permissible consent, so Ascend can look beyond a thin bureau file and underwrite on cash flow — a Consumer Report's CRA Base Report, Income Insights, Network Insights, and a LendScore — to approve responsibly and reduce credit risk.

**Story arc (short prose):**
Maya Torres applies for a $600 short-term loan. Her credit bureau file is thin — no reliable score — so a bureau-only decision would decline her or price the risk blindly. Ascend asks Maya to connect her bank. One Plaid Link Consumer Report session collects FCRA consent and the connection; Plaid generates the report asynchronously. Ascend reads the CRA Base Report (365-day cash flow, low NSFs), Income Insights (a verified, recurring income stream), Network Insights (favorable cross-network behavior — a budgeting-app connection, no cash-advance stacking; illustrative), and a LendScore (a cash-flow-backed risk score with reason codes). Combined with the thin bureau file, the cash-flow evidence supports a confident approval that bureau data alone could not — Ascend serves a real borrower and reduces its risk and exposure. Compliance-safe language only; happy path only.
